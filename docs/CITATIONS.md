# Academic Citations

## Primary Algorithm References

### Dynamic Programming and Memoization

1. Bellman, R. (1957). *Dynamic Programming*. Princeton University Press.
   - Foundational work establishing dynamic programming methodology

2. Michie, D. (1968). "Memo Functions and Machine Learning". *Nature*, 218, 19-22.
   - Original paper introducing memoization

3. Cormen, T.H., Leiserson, C.E., Rivest, R.L., & Stein, C. (2009). *Introduction to Algorithms* (3rd ed.). MIT Press.
   - Chapter 15: Dynamic Programming
   - Standard textbook reference for algorithm analysis

### Matrix Exponentiation

4. Gries, D., & Levin, G. (1980). "Computing Fibonacci Numbers (and Similarly Defined Functions) in Log Time". *Information Processing Letters*, 11(2), 68-69.
   - Formal treatment of O(log n) Fibonacci computation

5. Knuth, D.E. (1997). *The Art of Computer Programming, Volume 1: Fundamental Algorithms* (3rd ed.). Addison-Wesley.
   - Section 1.2.8: Fibonacci Numbers
   - Comprehensive treatment of Fibonacci mathematics

### Binet's Formula

6. Binet, J.P.M. (1843). "Mémoire sur l'intégration des équations linéaires aux différences finies, d'un ordre quelconque, à coefficients variables". *Comptes Rendus de l'Académie des Sciences de Paris*, 17, 559-567.
   - Original publication of the closed-form formula

7. de Moivre, A. (1718). *The Doctrine of Chances*.
   - Earlier derivation of the Binet-like formula

### Fast Doubling

8. Takahashi, D. (2000). "A Fast Algorithm for Computing Large Fibonacci Numbers". *Information Processing Letters*, 75(6), 243-246.

9. Nayuki. "Fast Fibonacci algorithms". https://www.nayuki.io/page/fast-fibonacci-algorithms
   - Excellent modern exposition of fast doubling
